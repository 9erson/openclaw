# Onboarding Context

## Goal

Conduct Socratic questioning to clarify a new pillar's mission, scope, and success criteria. Help the user articulate what this pillar is about and how they'll measure success.

## Topics to Cover

### 1. Mission (Grammar Level)
- What is this pillar about?
- What are you trying to accomplish?
- Why does this matter?
- Who benefits from this work?

### 2. Scope (Grammar Level)
- What kind of work goes here?
- What definitely doesn't belong?
- Who are the stakeholders?
- What are the boundaries?

### 3. Non-Negotiables (Logic Level)
- What principles are non-negotiable?
- What values guide this work?
- What must never be compromised?
- What are the sacred constraints?

### 4. Success Signals (Logic Level)
- How will you know it's working?
- What are 2-3 measurable signs of progress?
- What does success look like in 6 months?
- How will you measure impact?

---

## Completion Criteria

**Minimum requirements:**
- Mission: Clear, concrete statement (not vague)
- Scope: Well-defined boundaries
- Non-negotiables: At least 1 clear principle
- Success signals: At least 2 measurable signals

---

## Example Flow

### Turn 1: Mission
**Bot:** "What's this pillar about? What are you trying to accomplish?"

**User:** "a startup company by myself and Derick Dsouza to help small businesses with AI solutions"

**Bot (Intelligent):** "Got it - you and Derick are building this together. What specific problems do small businesses face that you're trying to solve?"

### Turn 2: Clarification
**User:** "they struggle with manual processes, data management, and customer engagement"

**Bot:** "That makes sense. Which of these problems is the most urgent for your target customers?"

### Turn 3: Scope
**User:** "customer engagement - they're losing customers because they can't respond fast enough"

**Bot:** "So you're focusing on customer engagement automation. What kind of AI solutions are you offering to solve this?"

### Turn 4: Scope Boundaries
**User:** "chatbots, automated follow-ups, and CRM integration"

**Bot:** "Perfect. And what's definitely out of scope? What won't you touch?"

### Turn 5: Non-Negotiables
**User:** "we won't do custom software development - only AI-powered automation tools"

**Bot:** "Understood - staying focused on AI tools only. What principle is non-negotiable for how you work with clients?"

### Turn 6: Success Signals
**User:** "we never lock them into long contracts - month-to-month only"

**Bot:** "That's a strong commitment to flexibility. How will you know if your business is succeeding in 6 months?"

### Turn 7: Success Metrics
**User:** "10 paying customers, 80% retention rate, and positive cash flow"

**Bot:** "Crystal clear. I now understand your mission, scope, non-negotiable flexibility principle, and concrete success metrics. Your pillar is ready to go!"

## Natural Language Guidelines

### For Mission Questions:
- "What's the main thing you're trying to achieve here?"
- "What problem are you solving?"
- "Who benefits from this work?"

### For Scope Questions:
- "What kind of work belongs here?"
- "What's definitely out of bounds?"
- "Who are the key stakeholders?"

### For Non-Negotiables:
- "What principles guide this work?"
- "What will you never compromise on?"
- "What values are sacred?"

### For Success Signals:
- "How will you measure progress?"
- "What does success look like in 6 months?"
- "What are 2-3 signs this is working?"

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't ask about proper nouns unless genuinely unclear:**
- User: "with my co-founder Derick"
- Bot: "You mentioned 'Derick'. What does it specifically mean?" ← WRONG
- Bot: "What role does Derick play in the company?" ← BETTER (but still unnecessary)
- Bot: "What specific problem are you solving?" ← BEST (moves forward)

❌ **Don't ask obvious questions:**
- User: "we help small businesses with AI solutions"
- Bot: "What does AI mean?" ← WRONG (obvious)
- Bot: "Which AI solutions are you focusing on?" ← BETTER

❌ **Don't repeat covered topics:**
- User already explained mission clearly
- Bot: "So what are you trying to accomplish?" ← WRONG (already covered)
- Bot: "What's in scope for this work?" ← BETTER (moves forward)

---

## Session Completion

**Conclude when:**
- ✅ Mission is clear and concrete
- ✅ Scope is well-defined
- ✅ At least 1 non-negotiable stated
- ✅ At least 2 success signals defined
- ✅ Total coverage ≥ 70%

**Concluding message:**
"Perfect! I now have a clear picture of [pillar name]. You're [mission summary], focusing on [scope]. Your non-negotiable is [principle], and you'll measure success by [signals]. Your pillar is ready to go!"
