## Response Pattern Analysis

**Challenge:** Minimal response activity logged across all domains despite 50+ questions.

### Possible Causes

1. **Question fatigue** — Too many questions across too many domains
2. **Timing** — Questions may have arrived during focused work periods
3. **Format** — Open-ended questions require more effort to answer
4. **Priorities** — Gerson may be executing on known tasks rather than responding to inquiries

### What Worked (Inferred from Domain Activity)

From manual.md observations:
- **Santa Casa:** "Prep questions work best when tied to specific upcoming sessions"
- **Bytebles:** "Gerson responds better to specific prep questions than general planning"
- **Acutis Flow:** "Questions that work: Status checks, scope clarification, role/responsibility mapping"

### What Didn't Work

- Repeating similar angles across domains (workshop prep, planning questions)
- Open-ended "what's the plan?" questions without time context
- Asking for information Gerson may not have yet (client details for non-active clients)

## Recommendations for Tomorrow

### 1. Reduce Question Volume

**Current:** ~8 questions per domain per day
**Proposed:** 2-3 strategic questions per domain, rotated through the day

**Rationale:** Quality over quantity. Gerson may be experiencing question fatigue.

### 2. Batch Related Questions

Instead of:
- "Workshop format?"
- "Prerequisites?"
- "Cheat sheet status?"

Try:
- "Workshop prep: what 3 things need to be ready by March 16?"

**Rationale:** Reduces cognitive load, allows comprehensive response.

### 3. Time-Box Questions

- **Morning (05:00-09:00 IST):** Planning questions for the day
- **Midday (12:00-14:00 IST):** Status checks on active work
- **Evening (17:00-19:00 IST):** Prep questions for tomorrow

**Rationale:** Aligns with natural workflow rhythms.

### 4. Use Daily Brief as Primary Interface

**Observation:** Daily brief consolidates key items into one digestible format.

**Proposal:** 
- Reduce orchestrator questions during active hours
- Let the brief surface key decisions/blockers
- Reserve questions for truly urgent items or when brief items go unaddressed for 2+ days

### 5. Direct Action Questions

Instead of: "What's the plan for Valiants launch?"
Try: "Valiants first event: should I draft a plan for your review, or do you have ideas already?"

**Rationale:** Offers concrete help vs. just asking for information.
