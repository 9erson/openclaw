**Date:** 2026-03-04 00:45 IST (19:15 UTC)

- Gerson was focused on execution vs. responding
- Questions landed during busy periods
- Question formulation needs improvement
- Question volume was too high

This learning task analyzes question patterns to identify improvements.

## Pattern Analysis

### 1. Question Volume is Too High

**Observation:** 46 questions in one day = ~4 questions per hour during active hours.

**Issue:** This creates notification fatigue. Even if each question is good individually, the cumulative effect is overwhelming.

**Recommendation:** 
- Reduce to 2-3 questions per domain per DAY (not per tick)
- Batch related questions together
- Use daily brief as primary interface for routine check-ins

### 2. Question Types Mix Strategic and Tactical

**Strategic questions** (good for periodic check-ins):
- "What's the goal/scope for Thursday Medi Guide session?"
- "Should we create a centralized client tracker?"
- "What's the vision for Valiants group activities?"

**Tactical questions** (better for daily brief):
- "Has the Consulate appointment been booked?"
- "Do you have the attendee list for the workshop?"
- "Is the cheat sheet ready?"

**Issue:** Mixing these in the same flow makes it hard to prioritize.

**Recommendation:**
- Strategic questions: 1-2 per domain per week (not per day)
- Tactical questions: Move to daily brief or queue system
- Distinguish question types in formulation

### 3. Timing Clustering

**Observation:** Questions were spread throughout the day (04:59 UTC to 17:00 UTC = 12 hours).

**Issue:** Questions arriving every 15 minutes creates constant interruption.

**Recommendation:**
- Batch questions into 2-3 delivery windows per day
- Morning batch (09:00 IST): Strategic/planning questions
- Afternoon batch (15:00 IST): Status/progress questions
- Evening batch (20:00 IST): Wrap-up/prep questions

### 4. Question Repetition Risk

**Observation:** Some questions were variations on the same theme:
- Bytebles: workshop prep asked 4 times in different ways (format, prerequisites, cheat sheets, polls)
- Acutis-flow: Medi Guide asked 4 times (domain, compensation, session goals, tracker)
- Knights: Valiants activities asked 3 times (activities, first event, backup plans)

**Issue:** While not technically repeating the same question, asking about the same topic from different angles multiple times in one day is effectively nagging.

**Recommendation:**
- Track question topics, not just exact wording
- Limit to ONE question per topic per day
- If no response, move to daily brief instead of asking again

### 5. Question Formulation Quality

**Good examples:**
- "Should we create a master tax coordination project or keep three separate country projects?" (specific, actionable)
- "Do you want to go to the Jio store today to transfer phone accounts?" (direct, time-bound)

**Weaker examples:**
- "What about Brand Electrify and Mera Genius?" (vague)
- "Any family matters this week?" (too open-ended)

**Issue:** Vague questions are harder to answer and easier to ignore.

**Recommendation:**
- Always include context: "Given X, should we Y?"
- Make questions specific and actionable
- Provide options when possible: "Option A or Option B?"

## Proposed Experiments

### Experiment 1: Volume Reduction
**Hypothesis:** Reducing to 2-3 questions per domain per day will improve response rate.

**Method:**
- Track question count per domain
- Limit orchestrator to max 3 questions per domain per day
- Measure response rate before/after

**Duration:** 3 days
**Success metric:** Response rate increases from baseline

### Experiment 2: Daily Brief as Primary Interface
**Hypothesis:** Moving routine/tactical questions to daily brief will reduce noise and improve response to strategic questions.

**Method:**
- Categorize questions as strategic vs tactical
- Move tactical questions to daily brief only
- Keep orchestrator questions strategic only

**Duration:** 3 days
**Success metric:** Strategic question response rate improves

### Experiment 3: Batched Delivery
**Hypothesis:** Delivering questions in 2-3 batches per day instead of every 15 minutes will reduce interruption and improve response rate.

**Method:**
- Collect questions during each window
- Deliver all at once at batch times (09:00, 15:00, 20:00 IST)
- Measure response rate

**Duration:** 3 days
**Success metric:** Response rate per question improves

## Key Learning

**Volume is the enemy of engagement.** Even good questions become noise when there are too many of them.

The orchestrator system is working as designed (asking strategic questions, not repeating exact wording), but the design itself needs adjustment: fewer questions, better batched, with routine items moved to daily brief format.
